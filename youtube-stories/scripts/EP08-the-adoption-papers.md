[pause 1s]

[pause 0.5s]

Nora read the name twice. [pause 1s] Then a third time. [pause 1s]

**[heavy, interior]**

Then she called the only person whose advice she trusted completely. [pause 0.5s] Not a friend. Not her mother. [pause 0.5s] Her own family law attorney, a woman named Cecile, who picked up on the second ring. [pause 1s]

### 🎬 BEAT 6 — THE PIVOT (5:30-7:00) — 318 words

**[controlled. deliberate.]**

Marcus came home at seven-fifteen. [pause 0.5s] Nora was at the kitchen table. [pause 0.5s] The Briarwood folder was open in front of her. [pause 1s]

He saw it before he set down his keys. [pause 1s] She watched his face go through four expressions in about two seconds. [pause 0.5s] She'd seen that sequence before — in her professional life, when a parent realized a caseworker already knew. [pause 1s]

Nora listened. [pause 0.5s] She did not interrupt. [pause 0.5s] When he finished, she asked one question. [pause 1s]

"Does Diane know you're still married to me?" [pause 2s]

He didn't answer. [pause 1s] That was her answer. [pause 2s]

Marcus asked her not to. He tried negotiating — they could figure this out privately, this didn't have to involve other people. [pause 1s]

Nora looked at him for a moment. [pause 1s]

"There is already a child," she said. [pause 0.5s] "Other people are already involved." [pause 2s]

A two-bedroom rental in a suburb he didn't choose. [pause 0.5s] A parenting schedule structured by a court order. [pause 0.5s] Child support obligations running alongside his own rent. [pause 0.5s] The divorce settlement had divided the equity in the house — but between the legal fees, the financial disclosure process, and the cost of establishing his solo household, there wasn't much equity left to divide. [pause 1s]

The licensing board inquiry didn't cost him his job. [pause 0.5s] But it cost him a contract. [pause 0.5s] The kind of contract that would have led to other contracts. [pause 0.5s] The kind of professional loss that doesn't make the news but changes the shape of a career permanently. [pause 1s]

He hadn't pursued any relationship since. [pause 1s]

He had run two lives simultaneously. [pause 0.5s] Now he could barely maintain one.
