## Description (copy-paste ready)

```
Seven years. Two thousand, five hundred and fifty-five lunches. She made every single one.

Claire was a school librarian who packed her husband Greg's lunch every morning at 6:30 AM — turkey on sourdough, no mustard, a green apple, and a brown paper bag folded exactly the way he liked it. She had a standing order at the farmers market. She never missed a day.

Greg was a downtown attorney who left the house at 7:45 every morning with that bag in his hand.

And every single day, that bag went directly into the office break room trash can.

When Claire found out — not just about the lunches, but about everything — she didn't scream. She didn't send screenshots. She didn't confront him at the office.

She planned.

Her Aunt Dorothy died and left her $42,000 and a handwritten note: "Do something that makes you impossible to ignore."

So she did.

This is the story of Two Thousand Five Hundred and Fifty-Five. A pale yellow food truck. A restaurant called Dorothy's. And the check for $12 that ended everything.

Karma always finds its way back. 🦋
```
