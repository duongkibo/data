## Description (copy-paste ready)

```
He booked the anniversary trip to save their marriage.

Maya had mentioned the Ashwood Inn exactly once — two years earlier, in passing. He remembered. He booked it. She thought it meant he had been listening all along.

He had not been.

At check-in, the concierge said: "Welcome back, Mr. Harlow." He navigated the hotel without the map. He walked straight to the corner table in the restaurant — the quietest one, least visible from the entrance. He knew the spa closed at eight, not nine.

Small things. Each one explainable. All of them wrong.

The next morning, the spa receptionist handed her the wrong booking confirmation. It was from twelve days earlier. "Couples deep tissue — second visit. Two guests. Daniel Harlow."

Maya asked to speak with the hotel manager.

She didn't leave early. She swam. She ordered room service. She tipped everyone. She photographed the confirmation before they crossed the county line. She emailed it to herself from the hotel lobby wifi.

By Thursday, she had five words for her attorney: "I want to document everything."

He kept the house. She kept it. He called the Ashwood Inn to complain. They logged the call. The judge referenced it in the ruling.

The phrase in the written decision: "not remorseful in character."

Every December, she sends a Christmas card to the hotel manager. Every year.

Karma always finds its way back. 🦋
```
