## Description (copy-paste ready)

```
She trusted her babysitter with her children.

She didn't think she needed to distrust her husband.

Emma was 23. A college student. She had been with the family for 14 months. Warm with the kids. Always left the kitchen cleaner than she found it.

Then Emma started arriving slightly more dressed up. Her daughter said "Emma stayed for dinner again." Marcus was always late on Thursdays — when Emma was there. Always a client, always a call, always somewhere else.

Dara opened her notes app on a Tuesday in February. She typed a date and two words: "Thursday. Late again."

She kept going. For six weeks. 18 entries.

The conference was a Thursday. She had a ticket. She had new shoes. On the morning of the event, she decided not to go. She didn't tell Marcus. She didn't tell Emma.

She came home at 7:14 PM. Both cars in the driveway.

She sat in her own car for eleven minutes.

Then she went in.

She walked to the kitchen. She found them. She set her purse on the counter. She said three words.

"Please leave now."

Her voice was completely level.

She sat at that table alone until morning.

The next morning, she called her sister. A week later, she sat across from an attorney named Patricia and opened her notes app. 18 entries. Dates. Times. Patterns.

Patricia said: "This is excellent documentation."

Dara said: "I know."

She also enrolled in the certification course she had shelved three years ago.

Emma got pregnant. Marcus had no plan for Emma. Her family cut contact. The divorce took 14 months — exactly as long as Emma had been their babysitter. Dara got primary custody. She kept the house (inheritance down payment, documented). Marcus: split three ways financially. The soccer coaching ended quietly.

She finished her certification in April. Got the job in June. Hired a new babysitter in September — through an agency, with references.

Every evening, she came home to her kids. She turned the light on in every room.

Karma always finds its way back. 🦋
```
