## Description (copy-paste ready)

```
Six months. Five a.m. Every single morning.

Mara was a senior project manager — methodical, precise, the kind of woman who color-coded her calendar and read the shape of a day before it started. When her husband Daniel announced he was finally going to take care of himself, she was proud.

He joined a gym twelve minutes from their house. He lost thirty pounds. He came home in his gym clothes every morning and went straight to the shower.

But his gym bag never smelled like a gym. He couldn't name a single exercise. And his water bottle — blue, insulated, full when he left — was always full when he came back.

Every single time.

One Tuesday morning, Mara drove to Velocity Fitness on Meridian Boulevard.

She asked the front desk if her husband Daniel Marsh was a member there.

The young man typed it in. Looked up. Typed it again.

There was no member named Daniel Marsh. There had never been a member named Daniel Marsh.

She sat in the parking lot for nine minutes. Then she drove home and started planning.

Mara was a project manager. She knew how to document. She knew how to prepare. And she knew — she had always known — that the house was in her name.

Karma always finds its way back. 🦋
```
