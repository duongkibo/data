## CHUNK 2 — Beats 5-7 (~920 words, ~5:30)

```
[heavy] The days after were the hardest. [pause 0.5s] Not because Grace was angry. [pause 0.5s] Because she was grieving something that hadn't died yet. [pause 1s]

At night, she lay beside him and stared at the ceiling. [pause 0.5s] She thought about the girl who'd married him at twenty-four. [pause 0.5s] How that girl had stood in front of a hundred people and said "forever" like she understood what the word meant. [pause 1s]

She replayed every moment. [pause 0.5s] The late nights at the office. The conferences that went an extra day. [pause 0.5s] The new cologne she'd noticed last spring and told herself was just Robert trying something different. [pause 1s]

[pause 2s]

[hopeful] Diane's office was on the fourteenth floor of a building downtown. [pause 0.5s] She had gray hair, reading glasses, and a reputation that made divorce attorneys in three counties nervous. [pause 1s]

Most states abolished these laws decades ago. [pause 0.5s] They argued it was wrong to use the legal system to punish sexual behavior. [pause 1s] But six states kept them. [pause 0.5s] North Carolina uses them the most. [pause 1s]

Diane filed two lawsuits that week. [pause 0.5s] One against Vanessa for alienation of affection. One against Robert for criminal conversation. [pause 1s] The evidence was Carl's binder. [pause 2s]

[satisfied] The trial lasted four days. [pause 1s] Robert hired a defense attorney from Raleigh. [pause 0.5s] Vanessa hired one from Charlotte. [pause 0.5s] Neither of them had ever lost a case quite like this. [pause 1s]

Grace took the stand on the second day. [pause 0.5s] She spoke for ninety minutes. [pause 0.5s] She didn't raise her voice once. [pause 1s] She described the twenty-eight years. The blueberry pancakes. The golden retriever. The receipt that fell from the suit pocket. [pause 0.5s] She described the Christmas party. [pause 1s] How Vanessa had looked her in the eye and called her marriage "beautiful." [pause 2s]

The jury had the case for forty-seven minutes. [pause 1s] Some of them were still writing when the foreperson stood up. [pause 1s]

They awarded Grace two point one million dollars from Vanessa — for alienation of affection. [pause 0.5s] And one point eight million from Robert — for criminal conversation. [pause 1s] Three point nine million dollars total. [pause 2s]

The courtroom went silent. [pause 0.5s] Robert stared at the table. [pause 0.5s] Vanessa started to cry. [pause 1s] Grace sat with her hands folded and didn't move. [pause 1s]

Forty-seven minutes. [pause 0.5s] That's how long it took a jury to decide what twenty-eight years of marriage was worth. [pause 2s]

[pause 0.5s] The practice he'd built over twenty-two years, gone in a single court order. [pause 1s] His dental license came under review from the state board — for conducting a sexual relationship with an employee at his practice. [pause 0.5s] An employee he supervised. [pause 1s]

Robert sold the family house. [pause 0.5s] Split the remainder with Grace in the divorce settlement. [pause 0.5s] Last anyone heard, he was working at a corporate dental chain in a strip mall, sharing an office with three other dentists, and renting a one-bedroom apartment above a dry cleaner. [pause 1s]

Grace hadn't raised her voice once.
```
