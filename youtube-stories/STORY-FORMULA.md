# 📐 Story Formula — 8-Beat Karma Caught Up

> **Mọi script Karma Caught Up phải tuân theo formula này. Không exception.**

---

## 🎯 The 8-Beat Structure (10-12 phút)

```
0:00-0:15   BEAT 1  HOOK
0:15-1:00   BEAT 2  PROTAGONIST INTRO
1:00-2:30   BEAT 3  THE CRACK
2:30-4:00   BEAT 4  THE BETRAYAL (25% mark — pain peak)
4:00-5:30   BEAT 5  THE FALL
5:30-7:00   BEAT 6  THE PIVOT (50% mark — KEY twist)
7:00-9:30   BEAT 7  KARMA STRIKES
9:30-10:30  BEAT 8  THE DOWNFALL
10:30-11:30 EPILOGUE
11:30-12:00 CTA
```

---

## 🎬 Beat-by-Beat Breakdown

### BEAT 1: HOOK (0:00-0:15)
**Goal**: Stop scroll trong 3 giây đầu.

**Formula**:
> "[Statement]. [Subverting twist]. [Curiosity gap]."

**Examples**:
- "She thought she'd buried it. Three years later, the universe disagreed."
- "He told everyone she was crazy. The wedding video told a different story."
- "She walked away with nothing. Six months later, she had everything he ever owned."

**Visual**: Dark moody opening shot. Single dramatic image. Music low.

**Voice tone**: Calm, slightly knowing. NOT angry.

---

### BEAT 2: PROTAGONIST INTRO (0:15-1:00)
**Goal**: Audience attach to protagonist.

**Formula**:
- Name (changed for privacy)
- Age + occupation (relatable)
- Current happy life
- 1 specific detail audience can picture

**Example**:
> "Sarah was 32. A nurse at Mercy Hospital. She'd been with Mark for 7 years, married for 3. Every morning she packed his lunch, exactly the way he liked it: turkey on rye, mustard, no pickles. They were happy. Or at least, that's what Sarah thought."

**Critical**: Show happiness BEFORE breaking it. Audience MUST root for her.

**Visual**: Warm domestic shots. Morning routine. Smile.

---

### BEAT 3: THE CRACK (1:00-2:30)
**Goal**: Plant seed of dread.

**Formula**:
- Small unusual moment
- Protagonist notices but dismisses
- Audience knows something's wrong
- Foreshadow without revealing

**Example**:
> "It started with a phone call she wasn't supposed to hear. Mark stepped onto the balcony, voice low. When Sarah asked who it was, he said 'wrong number.' She believed him. Of course she did. Then it was the late nights at work. The new cologne. The phone always face-down. Each one alone, nothing. Together, a pattern Sarah was too tired to see."

**Visual**: Subtle off-key shots. Phone face down. Empty side of bed. Whispered conversations.

**Voice tone**: Slower. Foreboding.

---

### BEAT 4: THE BETRAYAL (2:30-4:00) ⭐ **PAIN PEAK**
**Goal**: Devastate audience emotionally.

**Formula**:
- The discovery moment (specific, sensory)
- The realization (slow burn)
- The confrontation (or no confrontation — sometimes worse)
- The full picture revealed

**Example**:
> "Sarah found out on a Tuesday. It wasn't dramatic. No lipstick on collar, no perfume on shirt. It was a single text on Mark's phone he'd left charging on the kitchen counter. From a name she didn't recognize. With words she'd never forget. 'I love you. Tell her tonight.' Tell her. Like Sarah was a problem to be managed. Like 7 years could be reduced to a notification."

**Visual**: Phone glow. Trembling hands. Empty face. Slow zoom.

**Voice tone**: Almost whispered. Devastating quiet.

---

### BEAT 5: THE FALL (4:00-5:30)
**Goal**: Audience cry WITH protagonist.

**Formula**:
- Immediate reaction (shock)
- The slow grief
- The questioning herself
- Hit rock bottom

**Example**:
> "The next 3 weeks were a blur. Sarah didn't eat. She didn't sleep. She moved through her days like a ghost wearing her own face. She thought about what she did wrong. The way she'd gained weight after the miscarriage. The promotion she took that meant longer hours. Was it her? Was she not enough? She replayed every moment, looking for the crack. And then one night at 3am, she stopped looking."

**Visual**: Dark room. Insomnia shots. Mirror reflection unrecognized. Rain.

**Voice tone**: Heavy. Slower. Pause more.

---

### BEAT 6: THE PIVOT (5:30-7:00) ⭐ **KEY TWIST**
**Goal**: Subvert revenge expectation. The DECISION.

**Formula**:
- Moment of clarity
- Decision NOT to react
- Walk away with dignity
- Set boundaries firmly

**Example**:
> "At 3am, Sarah did something unexpected. She opened her laptop and started writing. Not a confrontation. Not a revenge plan. A list. Of everything she wanted her life to look like. Without him. Without the pain. Without the rage. By morning, she'd packed two suitcases. By noon, she'd called a lawyer. By evening, she was on a plane to Portland — a city she'd always wanted to visit. She didn't tell Mark she was leaving. She didn't say goodbye. She just... walked away. And the universe was watching."

**Visual**: Sunrise. Packing. Calm decision. Empty house. Plane window.

**Voice tone**: Lift slightly. Hope enters.

**THIS IS THE KEY MOMENT** — what makes channel unique. Protagonist chooses peace. NOT revenge.

---

### BEAT 7: KARMA STRIKES (7:00-9:30)
**Goal**: Slow-burn satisfaction. Universe does the work.

**Formula**:
- 3-5 specific karma events
- Build progressively
- Each independent (not protagonist's doing)
- Antagonist's choices destroy themselves

**Example**:
> "Mark proposed to her three months later. The other woman. The one from the text. She said yes. They had a small wedding — Mark couldn't afford a big one anymore, since Sarah had cleaned out the joint accounts that were legally hers. He didn't fight it. The lawyers told him he'd lose. Six months in, his new wife discovered his messages with someone else. She'd been on his work trips. Apparently, his pattern was the pattern. She left. Took half. Then his job. Mark had been running personal errands during work hours — meeting his new wife when he was supposedly at meetings. HR found out. They didn't fire him gently. Then his apartment. The one he'd moved into after the divorce, with the woman he'd traded Sarah for. Mark couldn't afford it alone. He moved back in with his mother at 38. And his mother — God bless her — never stopped reminding him whose fault it was."

**Visual**: Wedding photos darkening. Empty office desk. Moving boxes. Mother's house.

**Voice tone**: Knowing satisfaction. Slight smile in voice.

---

### BEAT 8: THE DOWNFALL (9:30-10:30)
**Goal**: Maximum catharsis. Antagonist hits bottom.

**Formula**:
- Final blow that breaks them
- Public humiliation (subtle)
- Acknowledge their own fault
- Karma fully complete

**Example**:
> "Two years after Sarah left, Mark sent her an email. Subject line: 'I'm sorry.' She didn't open it. She didn't have to. She'd heard from mutual friends what happened. The new wife had taken everything. His job had blacklisted him in the industry. He was living in his mother's basement, working at a hardware store, and couldn't afford a divorce attorney. Karma hadn't asked Sarah to lift a finger. The universe had a long memory, and it remembered every lie Mark told."

**Visual**: Unopened email notification. Empty hardware store. Basement room.

**Voice tone**: Calm finality. The story complete.

---

### EPILOGUE (10:30-11:30)
**Goal**: Show protagonist's peaceful new life.

**Formula**:
- Time skip ("Two years later...")
- New life details
- Peace + happiness
- No mention of antagonist

**Example**:
> "Sarah is 34 now. She lives in Portland. She runs a small wellness studio. She got remarried last year — to a man who packs HIS own lunch, kisses her every morning, and never leaves his phone face down. She still thinks about Mark sometimes. Not with anger. Just with quiet gratitude. Because the worst day of her life turned out to be the door that opened to the best one."

**Visual**: Sunny morning. New home. Smile. Happiness real.

**Voice tone**: Warm. Settled. Peace.

---

### CTA (11:30-12:00)
**Goal**: Subscribe + comment.

**Formula**:
> "If you've ever walked away from someone who hurt you, leave a 🦋 in the comments. Every butterfly is someone who chose peace over revenge. Subscribe for more stories where karma caught up. Because sometimes, the best revenge... is no revenge at all."

**Visual**: Butterfly graphic. Subscribe overlay. Channel branding.

**Voice tone**: Warm invitation.

---

## ⚖️ Story Writing Rules

### DO
- ✅ Use specific sensory details (sound, smell, texture)
- ✅ Use real time markers (3am, Tuesday, 7 years)
- ✅ Show, don't tell ("She didn't eat" vs "She was sad")
- ✅ Pause for breath markers `[pause 1s]`
- ✅ Vary sentence length (short. then longer descriptive sentences for rhythm.)
- ✅ Include 1 surprising detail (turkey on rye, no pickles)
- ✅ Names neutral but memorable
- ✅ End with peace, not anger

### DON'T
- ❌ Real names
- ❌ Recognizable locations
- ❌ Graphic descriptions
- ❌ Active revenge
- ❌ Justify protagonist's anger
- ❌ Long monologues (audience tunes out)
- ❌ Overuse "she felt" or "she thought" (show via action)
- ❌ Wrap up too neatly (real life is messy)

## 📊 Word count target

| Beat | Words | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1 Hook | 25-40 | Punchy |
| 2 Intro | 100-150 | Establish character |
| 3 Crack | 200-280 | Build dread slowly |
| 4 Betrayal | 200-280 | Devastate |
| 5 Fall | 180-240 | Grief |
| 6 Pivot | 250-320 | KEY moment + decision |
| 7 Karma | 350-450 | Slow burn |
| 8 Downfall | 150-200 | Final blow |
| Epilogue | 130-180 | Peace |
| CTA | 80-100 | Subscribe |
| **Total** | **1,665-2,240 words** | **10-12 min @ ~150wpm slow** |

## 🎙️ Pacing Markers

ElevenLabs needs pause markers for natural delivery:
- `[pause 0.5s]` — within sentences
- `[pause 1s]` — between thoughts
- `[pause 2s]` — between major beats (key moments)
- `[pause 3s]` — before THE BETRAYAL reveal (Beat 4)

Use `[whispers]` for intimate moments (Beat 4, 5).
Use `[knowing]` for karma reveals (Beat 7).
Use `[warm]` for epilogue (Beat 9, CTA).
